Thalhe Festival

 Welcome to Thalhe, a dance festival exclusively dedicated to adults. This vibrant and engaging celebration of artistic expression through movement was conceived with the vision of providing an inclusive and inspiring platform for dance enthusiasts, where the diversity of styles and passion for movement converge in a unique spectacle.

 Our aim is to create an environment that not only enhances participants' dance skills but also fosters a cohesive community where the exchange of experiences and the celebration of the art of dance are fundamental pillars. We are committed to crafting an experience that challenges and inspires, while nurturing the individual and collective growth of all involved.

 Get ready for an exciting journey, where dance transcends its boundaries, and artistic expression finds a vibrant and welcoming home.

Mission

 To create a welcoming and integrative space for dance lovers to express their art. To contribute to the promotion of culture, well-being, and creative expression by providing a memorable event that will leave a lasting impact on the lives of all involved.

Values
  • Inclusion
  • Diversity

Vision

 To be a world-renowned festival for adult dance, present in major cities across various countries. We aim to create an environment where dance becomes a universal language that connects hearts and minds, celebrating the diversity and richness of each participant's experiences.

  Our vision is to be a beacon of creativity, promoting collaboration, learning, and the appreciation of dance as a powerful form of human expression. By designing a welcoming and inspiring environment, we aspire to be an event that not only delights the senses but also leaves a lasting mark, inspiring a passionate dance community to flourish and thrive.

History

 The current dance world is increasingly evolving and incorporating adult ballet. Studios have been expanding their class offerings, and clothing and accessory companies have been adapting by creating special lines to meet this demand.

 Adults seek ballet practice not only for its physical and mental health benefits but also as a form of leisure and social integration. This growth has also created space for those who began their studies as children or teenagers but did not pursue a professional dance career due to various reasons. These reasons include limited job opportunities, low wages, challenging aesthetic standards, injuries, and especially the age restrictions imposed in auditions.

 With 30 years of experience and many comings and goings, we have observed a significant increase in the demand for adult ballet classes. In the past, when we stopped, there was no place to practice what our bodies were accustomed to. Nowadays, we can find some studios offering these opportunities, but something very special is still missing... THE STAGE. The space for these adult ballet lovers who, besides participating in classes, also dream of being on stage.

 It is with this in mind that the Thalhe Festival was born. It aims to create an integrative and welcoming space for young adults, adults, and seniors who wish to experience being on stage and showcasing their art. Whether it is their first experience, their thousandth, or performing a style or variation they have always dreamed of. What we know as artists is that the stage is "the icing on the cake" for all the effort put into the classroom. At the Thalhe Festival, dancers will have the opportunity to experience everything a professional dancer does, including warm-up classes, physiotherapy sessions, rehearsal coaches, photo sessions, and more.

Name

 The name "Thalhe" is inspired by the Portuguese word for carving, a term deeply rooted in carpentry with indigenous origins. Carving is a craft where the artisan uses tools, time, and dedication to transform raw wood into a specific shape or design. Drawing on this analogy, the founder of Grupo Thalhe believes that, just as wood can be carved and shaped, all bodies can be molded and refined through advanced techniques in classical ballet. This vision aims to create opportunities for children and young people who might have been overlooked due to not having the "ideal" body type, and to share this inclusive perspective with future generations.

Rayana Fonte

  I am a classical ballerina with a solid foundation in the Russian Vaganova method, enriched by the French method under the guidance of the renowned Maitre Jorge Texeira, current director of the Municipal Theater of Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Additionally, I am a choreographer, director, and founder of RFDC, as well as the CEO of the Thalhe Festival.

 My passion for dance has led me on a journey of continuous learning. I am a certified teacher by the American Ballet Theatre (ABT) in the Cecchetti method and have also completed the International Independent Study Program at Steps On Broadway in New York, covering various dance styles including tap, jazz, modern, hip-hop, and contemporary.

 Throughout my career, I have been recognized with awards in national and international dance competitions.

 Furthermore, I have had the opportunity to participate in a variety of artistic projects, from television programs to music videos with celebrities, films, car shows, circuses, renowned amusement parks, TV commercials, cruises, parades, and other events.

  My professional journey has taken me around the world, sharing my art in locations such as China, Hong Kong, Macau, New Zealand, Italy, Portugal, South Africa, Bolivia, Chile, Mexico, Canada, Greece, Turkey, Croatia, Spain, the United States, and Sint Maarten.

  With each experience, I strive to continue growing as an artist and to inspire others through dance.

  My journey began early on when I discovered the magic of classical and modern dance. Over the years, I have honed my technique in styles such as tap, jazz, contemporary, and urban dance, allowing me to transition between different forms of expression with grace and fluidity.

  As a choreographer, I constantly seek to challenge the boundaries of creativity, exploring new narratives and concepts through movement. My work reflects a fusion of tradition and innovation, incorporating contemporary elements into performances that captivate and inspire.

 In addition to my stage career, I am also a dedicated educator, sharing my passion for dance with students of all ages.

  I believe in the transformative power of art and the positive impact it can have on our lives.

 I am always on the lookout for new opportunities for collaboration and artistic exploration. If you share my passion for dance and wish to embark on this creative journey together, I am eager to connect and create something extraordinary.

Thais Sarmento

 Thais Sarmento began her dance studies in classical ballet at the age of 8 in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Under the guidance of Jorge Texeira, she achieved soloist and principal dancer positions with Grupo Thalhe. Throughout her career, Thais has participated in numerous contests across Brazil, consistently earning awards and recognition.

 Thais has performed with renowned dance partners, including Andre Valadon (National Dance Alive Ballet, USA), Welligton Gomes (Rio de Janeiro's Municipal Theater), Reginaldo Oliveira (Salzburger Landestheater, Germany), William Pedro (Bejart), Daniel Deivison (San Francisco Ballet), and Alysson Rocha (Ballett Dortmund). She completed her graduation at Petite Danse, becoming a certified professional teacher.

 As a soloist with Companhia Brasileira de Ballet, Thais performed in various ballets such as Coppelia, Nutcracker, Don Quixote, Paquita, La Fille Mal Gardée, Giselle, Le Corsaire, La Esmeralda, Grand Pas Classique, and numerous Balanchine ballets.

 In 2018, Thais relocated to the United States with her family, obtaining permanent residence through the EB2-NIW Visa. After a hiatus, she returned to dance in 2020, joining Relevé-tios Dance Company. Thais has performed as a guest artist in various local studio shows and events, including The Steinbrenner Family Foundation Children’s Christmas Concerts with The Florida Orchestra.

 In addition to her dance career, Thais is a dedicated businesswoman, holding multiple college degrees in Graphic and Industrial Design and Business Administration. She also works as a ballet teacher and coach, continuing to inspire and train the next generation of dancers.

Thalhe Festival Rules and Regulations

 We encourage all Thalhe Festival participants, and teachers to check the website and to follow on all social media platforms for the latest news, live-streaming of the events, registration, information and other updates.

 For the health and safety of our participants, we follow all guidelines issued by the U.S. government, state and local/city governments, and theater policies.

 All participants and accompany are request to sign a waiver and guide accordance for a participation.

 The Thalhe Dance Festival is open to dance students, amateurs and professionals of all nationalities, ages 18 years old and up.

 Participants are welcome to register at multiple category, however, The Thalhe Festival reserves the right to restrict the number of events a participant attends, or decline any registration at discretion (subject to space availability at each category).
